# ONDC:FIS10 2.1.0 — Overview

## Summary

This domain enables users to buy and manage gift cards directly from providers. It ensures that gift card purchases are smoothly processed, with real-time updates on purchase status, fulfillment, and recipient details, giving both buyers and sellers confidence in the transaction.

## Sector & Purpose

The sector is **FIS (Financial Services)** — where people buy, sell, or give gift cards as a form of payment or appreciation. The core problem this domain solves is the lack of a seamless, transparent, and trustworthy way for consumers to purchase gift cards without relying on a single, centralized platform. With FIS10, users can buy gift cards directly from providers, and those providers can instantly confirm, update, and track purchases — all in a way that works across different stores, apps, and locations.

## Real-World Actors

The people actually buying and using this service are **regular consumers** — like someone buying a gift card for a friend or family member. On the other side, **gift card providers** — such as retail chains, digital platforms, or financial institutions — are the ones offering and fulfilling the gift cards. These providers manage the actual issuance, delivery, and tracking of the cards, and now can do so with real-time visibility and user control.

## Use Cases

- A user buys a gift card online and enters a code to confirm the purchase.
- A user purchases a physical gift card in a store and gets instant confirmation.
- A user updates the recipient’s name before finalizing a gift card purchase.
- A seller cancels a gift card order after a customer requests it.
- A user completes a gift card purchase by confirming details and receiving a fulfillment code.

## Key Concepts

- **Gift-card**: A prepaid card that can be used for purchases, often given as a gift.
- **ONDC**: The open network that connects buyers and sellers across different platforms and locations.
- End-to-end fulfillment: From purchase initiation to confirmation and status update, all steps are tracked and validated.
- Recipient update: Users can change who the gift card is sent to before it’s finalized.
- Cancellation: Sellers can cancel a gift card purchase if needed, within the transaction lifecycle.
