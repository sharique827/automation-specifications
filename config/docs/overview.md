# ONDC:RET12 1.2.5 — Overview

## Summary
ONDC:RET12 covers the fashion retail domain, focused on buying and selling clothing online. It enables consumers to discover, order, receive, return, replace, or cancel fashion purchases from retail clothing stores, especially helping offline stores come online and reach buyers digitally.

## Sector & Purpose
This domain sits in the fashion retail sector. Its purpose is to help offline clothing stores participate in online commerce, so customers can shop fashion items from more retail sellers without those stores having to depend only on large e-commerce platforms.

## Real-World Actors
In plain business terms, this domain is about consumers buying from retail clothing stores. The main transacting parties are:
- Customers looking for clothing and fashion options online
- Local or offline retail clothing stores putting their catalog and inventory online
- Sellers and delivery operations supporting fulfillment, updates, returns, replacements, and cancellations

## Use Cases
- Discovering fashion and clothing products from retail stores
- Browsing full or incremental fashion catalogs
- Placing clothing orders for delivery
- Paying through cash on delivery
- Receiving delivery updates and sharing buyer instructions
- Handling buyer-side cancellations
- Handling seller-side cancellations
- Managing out-of-stock situations
- Requesting returns for clothing purchases
- Canceling a return request
- Processing replacements for fashion items
- Handling customer grievance or issue-management cases during delivery flows

## Key Concepts
- Online fashion retail, especially clothing sold by retail stores
- Bringing offline clothing stores online so they can transact digitally
- Consumer-to-retail-store commerce in the fashion sector
- Post-order lifecycle handling such as delivery, cancellation, return, and replacement

## Example Scenario
A customer wants to buy clothing and checks options from nearby or local retail stores that are now available online through this domain. Instead of being limited to a few large platforms, the customer gets more choices from local stores, compares available fashion items, places an order, and receives the products through delivery. If something goes wrong, such as an out-of-stock item, a cancellation, a return, or a replacement need, the domain supports those retail interactions as part of the same fashion buying journey.
