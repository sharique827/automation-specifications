# ONDC:RET18 1.2.5 — Overview

## Summary
ONDC:RET18 is a retail domain enabling health and wellness transactions—the buying and selling of medicines, supplements, and wellness products online. It connects consumers searching for health products with retailers and practitioners, supporting the full transaction lifecycle from product discovery through delivery, returns, and issue resolution.

## Sector & Purpose
This domain operates in the health and wellness retail sector, resolving a core problem: enabling seamless, standardized commerce for health products across a distributed network of retailers. Consumers increasingly buy medicines, vitamins, supplements, and wellness items online; this domain provides the infrastructure for retailers of all sizes to participate in that market through a single, interoperable standard rather than building separate platforms.

## Real-World Actors
The transacting parties are straightforward:
- **Consumers**: people buying health and wellness products for personal or household use
- **Retailers**: health shops, pharmacies, supplement sellers, and wellness stores that stock and sell these products online

The domain orchestrates their interactions without intermediaries needing to build proprietary integrations.

## Use Cases
- Browsing and discovering health products across multiple retailers
- Purchasing medicines, supplements, and wellness items with multiple payment methods including cash-on-delivery
- Scheduled delivery slots for health product orders
- Self-pickup from local retail locations
- Cancelling or returning orders if plans change or products don't meet expectations
- Replacing defective or incorrect items
- Resolving disputes or issues through integrated grievance management
- Combining items from multiple categories (e.g., medicines and health foods) in a single cart and checkout

## Key Concepts
- **Health Products**: medicines, supplements, vitamins, and wellness-related retail goods
- **Order Fulfillment**: multiple delivery modes (home delivery, slotted delivery, self-pickup) and inventory management
- **Payment Flexibility**: support for cash-on-delivery and other payment methods to serve diverse consumer preferences
- **Returns & Replacements**: post-delivery product management and resolution workflows
- **Catalog Management**: incremental and full catalog discovery to keep product information current across the network

## Example Scenario
A consumer places an order for health-conscious food products (such as fish) from a wellness retailer. They select a convenient delivery slot from the retailer's available options. The order is confirmed with cash-on-delivery as payment. The retailer picks and packs the order. When delivery is attempted, the driver updates the buyer in real-time. Upon receipt, if the product doesn't meet expectations, the buyer can initiate a return or request a replacement—all tracked and resolved within the same system that enabled the original transaction.
