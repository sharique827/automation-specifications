# ONDC:RET13 1.2.5 — Overview

## Summary

ONDC:RET13 enables the digital sale and distribution of beauty and personal care products through the ONDC network. It connects consumers with small cosmetic shops and sellers, allowing independent retailers to list inventory and fulfill orders directly without building their own e-commerce platform.

## Sector & Purpose

The beauty and personal care sector—cosmetics, skincare, hygiene products, and personal grooming items—has traditionally been dominated by large online retailers and brand-owned channels. This domain solves a critical problem: **enabling small cosmetic shops and independent beauty retailers to participate in digital commerce and reach customers beyond their physical store location**. Rather than competing solely on physical location or investing heavily in their own platform, these retailers can list products on ONDC-enabled seller apps and access a network of buyers and logistics providers.

## Real-World Actors

- **Consumers**: End buyers searching for and purchasing beauty and personal care products
- **Cosmetic store owners / small beauty retailers**: Businesses with physical shops or small inventory who want to reach online customers
- **ONDC-enabled seller platforms (BPPs)**: Applications that aggregate and list products from multiple cosmetic stores
- **Logistics providers**: Courier services (e.g., Blue Dart) that handle order fulfillment and delivery

## Use Cases

- Consumers discovering and browsing beauty products across multiple small retailers in one interface
- Small cosmetic shops listing inventory without building their own website or app
- Cash-on-delivery purchases of beauty items
- Cross-category shopping (combining beauty products with other items in a single order)
- Order cancellations and returns for cosmetic purchases
- Handling out-of-stock scenarios when a specific product is unavailable
- Returns and replacements for damaged or defective beauty items
- Dispute resolution (IGM) when issues arise with delivery or product quality

## Key Concepts

- **Beauty and personal care products**: Cosmetics, skincare, shampoos, soaps, deodorants, and similar items
- **Cosmetic store inventory**: Physical or digital stock maintained by small retailers
- **ONDC-enabled seller platform**: The software layer that lists products and manages orders for multiple stores
- **Fulfillment and logistics**: The process of picking, packing, and delivering orders through courier partners
- **Catalog management**: The ability for stores to keep product lists fresh with full, incremental, and refresh-pull updates

## Example Scenario

A consumer browsing an ONDC-enabled shopping app discovers a beauty cream listed by a small local cosmetic store. They add it to their cart along with a complementary skincare product, select cash-on-delivery as payment, and place the order. The cosmetic store receives the order through the ONDC network, picks and packs the items, and hands them to Blue Dart or another courier service for delivery. The consumer receives the package at their address, inspects it, and pays the delivery agent upon receipt. If the product arrives damaged, the consumer can initiate a return through the app, and the store processes a replacement or refund.
