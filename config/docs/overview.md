# ONDC:FIS13 2.0.0 — Overview

## Summary

ONDC:FIS13 enables consumers to discover, compare, and purchase insurance products directly through digital networks. It standardizes how insurance providers list their policies and how consumers navigate the entire journey from discovering insurers to completing a purchase—covering accidental injury, hospital cash, and travel protection insurance.

## Sector & Purpose

**Sector:** Insurance

This domain solves a critical friction point in insurance distribution: consumers have limited ways to discover and purchase insurance products outside traditional agent channels or direct insurer websites. By creating a standardized commerce network, ONDC:FIS13 allows insurance providers to reach customers through multiple digital pathways, and it gives consumers transparent access to compare insurance options for life's unexpected events—accidents, hospitalization, and travel emergencies—in a single marketplace experience.

## Real-World Actors

- **Consumers:** Individuals seeking protection against accidental injury, hospitalization costs, or travel-related incidents
- **Insurance Providers:** Companies that underwrite and manage insurance policies, listing their offerings and processing customer purchases through the network
- **Insurance Intermediaries:** Platforms or aggregators that help consumers navigate policies and simplify the purchase process

## Use Cases

- **Accidental Insurance:** Consumers discover and purchase coverage for accidental injuries, with insurers managing claims and benefits
- **Hospicash Insurance:** Consumers obtain coverage for hospitalization-related expenses, helping mitigate costs during medical emergencies
- **Transit Insurance:** Consumers buy protection for incidents occurring during travel or transit, providing peace of mind while commuting or journeying

## Key Concepts

- **Master Policies:** Standardized insurance product templates that insurers publish; consumers browse and select from these foundational offerings
- **Product Discovery:** The ability to find available insurance policies from multiple providers, filtered by type (accidental, hospitalization, transit) and other parameters
- **Purchase Journey:** The complete flow from discovering an insurer, viewing policy details, applying, and completing the transaction
- **Policy Selection & Customization:** Consumers compare terms, coverage limits, and premiums across providers before purchasing
- **Balance & Fulfillment:** Tracking policy activation, managing digital receipts, and handling errors that may arise during fulfillment

## Example Scenario

A consumer realizes they need travel insurance before a weekend trip. They access an ONDC-enabled marketplace and search for "transit insurance." The platform displays policies from multiple insurance providers, each showing coverage limits, premiums, and claim processes. The consumer compares three options, reads the detailed terms of their preferred policy, and completes the purchase in minutes. The policy is activated immediately, and they receive a digital receipt. If a billing issue arises, the error-handling flow ensures it's flagged and resolved before the trip begins, so the consumer travels with confidence and protection in place.
